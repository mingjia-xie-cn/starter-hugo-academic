  (Job Market Paper)

abstract: I examine heterogeneous returns to education attainment (high school
  degree or above) in China by exploiting the variation in the educational
  attainment caused by a reform which increases the compulsory education
  schooling years with different implementation dates across provinces. Using
  data from China Household Finance Survey (CHFS), I find that the individual
  with higher resistance to the educational attainment (or less likely to go to
  high school and any further education program) due to both the observable and
  unobserved characteristics are with higher return to education. One
  explanation for the pattern of reverse selection on gain is that children who
  are less likely to be enrolled in the education system have more disadvantaged
  backgrounds, thus with the lower income without educational attainment.
  Education acts like an equalizer that reduces the income gap along the
  distribution of the likelihood to educational attainment, generating the
  larger return to education for children who are less likely to have
  educational attainment.
