publication: China Economic Review
abstract: "The oil product pricing mechanism is a regulation system that was
  introduced in the late 1990s to control the oil product prices in China. For
  the first time to our knowledge, we provide an empirical evaluation of this
  regulation system, with a particular focus on its role in China's
  macroeconomy. Based on monthly data between 2000 and 2013, we find that: (i)
  contrary to the general public's impression, the mechanism is ‘fair’ overall
  in the sense that it responds to the rise and fall of international oil price
  symmetrically; (ii) the effect of the mechanism on the Chinese economy,
  however, is very limited; and (iii) the limited effect of the mechanism holds
  for different levels of regulation during the studied period, suggesting that
  potential deregulation may have little impact on the economy."
